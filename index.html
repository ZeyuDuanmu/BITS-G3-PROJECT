Samantha Yazzie

Samantha Yazzie is a full scholarship recipient. However, she frets about the $20,000 she had to borrow — not for tuition, but for housing during her freshman year alone. Because of the COVID-19, Yazzie cannot earn enough money to afford the expend. So, she worried about how to pay it back. Faced with such financial problems, Yazzie, a stellar student, might have to give up on going to university.

Diane Yeung

Yeung grew up with a brother and an immigrant single mom, her family income dipping below the poverty line for much of her childhood. Experiencing bouts of poverty in Yeung's youth caused what psychologists call trauma due to poverty. Yeung graduated high school and enrolled at a university. She attended a state university that was at least four times the price of a community college, and still had to work despite having two scholarships and financial aid. She worked as a busboy on the weekends, commuting hours from campus to the city, only to make $4 an hour before tips. However, due to the pandemic, she has now lost her job and can no longer afford to go to university.
